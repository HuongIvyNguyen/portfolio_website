About Me

My name is Ivy Huong Nguyen. I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in analytical and computational chemistry at the University of Washington, Seattle campus. I found my passion for data analytics while conducting one of my two main Ph.D. projects which involves studying the dynamics of biological complexes formed in the gas phase. Beyond my major Ph.D. projects, I have also taken numerous courses that are related to Statistics, Data Science, Experimentation Design, and Machine Learning to complement to my data analysis skills. I am interested in using various programming techniques to answer challenging scientific and non-scientific questions. I enjoy thinking and doing problems related to mathematics, physics, chemistry, and computer programming.

A/B Testing a New Menu Launch

A/B Test

More Details

An upscale coffee chain wants to initiate a new feature into their menu which would introduce gourmet sandwiches with a limited wine offerings. The manager believes that television advertising campaign would be an effective way to drive more customers into the stores to drive the new feature. However, television advertising campaign is quite costly and thus A/B testing would be a great way to test out whether the new feature would benefit the company. The test is ran for 12 weeks in Denver and Chicago. Trend and seasonality variables are created during the A/B test. Intercorrelation is also tested among predicting variables to avoid overestimation in the analysis.

Identifying Fraud from the Enron Email Dataset

Machine Learning

The goal of this project was to utilize the financial and email dataset obtained from the Enron scandal to build a predictive analytic model that could identify a person of interest (POI). This project successfully built a “person of interest” (POI) identifier using DecisionTreeClassifier algorithm from scikit-learn with the optimized parameters. The tuned parameters were obtained by using GridSearchCV module from scikit-learn. Other algorithms such as SVC, Naive Bayes, Kmeans Clustering and Adaboost were also tested during the processing of choosing the best algorithm. The identifier worked efficiently with the chosen algorithm and the optimized parameters given the resulting averaged metrics such as precision score and recall score./p>
